Honoring Our Veterans

Silas Johnson Jr. Tribute by Enzo Alexander

Name:

Sailor Silas Johnson Jr.

Years Served:

1944-1945

Branch of Military:

The Navy

Brief History:

Born in 1926, son of Silas Johnson Sr., he only turned of age to get involved in WWII during it's final years. Once he was old enough, he had Boot Camp ar the Great Lakes Navy Facility. He was placed on a supply ship, that was later sold as scrap metal, and held the rank of Sailor and was never promoted. On the Supply ship he got a tatoo of his initials on his left arm, and he also got a case of Yellow Jaundace. In 1945, he was honorably discharged, and later found his future wife.

Thank-you Message

Thank You, Silas Johnson Jr., If not for Veterans like you, vital supplies wouldn't have gotten to where they were needed. Your role was still vital to WWII. So, Thank you for being a Sailor on a Supply ship, we are trully greatful.

"The Navy has both a tradition and a future ─ and we look with pride and confidence in both directions." - Admiral George Anderson, CNO, 1 August 1961.
